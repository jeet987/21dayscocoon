Vinyasa Yoga

Vinyasa flow is a style of yoga where the poses are synchronized with the breath in a continuous rhythmic flow.

Hatha Yoga

Hatha is a blanket term for many different ‘styles’ and schools that use the body as a means for self-inquiry.

Ashtanga Yoga

The practitioner moves at the pace of her own breath and to her personal edge, or growth point.

Yin Yoga

It is a type of yoga with roots in martial arts as well as yoga, and it’s designed to increase circulation in the joints and improve flexibility.

Iyengar Yoga

Iyengar yoga is usually less intense than other types of yoga, it’s suitable for people of all ages and skill levels..

What Is Yoga?

Yoga is the union between the body, mind, and spirit.

It is a place of discovery and connection with your own body that encompasses balance,

proper stretching techniques, breathing, meditation, centering the mind and spirit — that’s yoga in its real form.

However, you’ll find that every type of yoga has a slightly different definition or interpretation.

Yoga ignores the “no pain, no gain” philosophy that’s rife in fitness communities.

Yoga is not a place to push through, go beyond your edge, or ignore your body.

The primary tenet is ahimsa, or non-harming, and that starts with choosing the right type of yoga for you.
